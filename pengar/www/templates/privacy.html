Privacy Since you probably want to know.

  • Email
  • Password
  • Transactions
  • Accounts
  • Social security number
  • Security code

Email Canonical identifier

Your email address will be stored in the Pengar database to enable you to log in using it together with your password.

Password Sssh, secret

Your password is used together with your email to authenticate you to the Pengar service.

Your password will be encrypted with a one-way hashing algorithm and stored in the Pengar database.

Transactions Do I really spend that much on food?

Your transactions contain the following information:

note
The text that is visible in the account overview on your online banking pages.
date
The date on which the transaction was registered.
amount
The amount of the transaction.

Transactions are stored in the database indefinitely.

Transactions are queried by the Pengar application and it is the transactions that give us the base for the beautiful graphs.

Accounts I should really get a savings account

Your account records contain the following information:

label
The name of the account
amount
The amount of the account when it was first added (we don't use this really, we should get rid of it).

The accounts are fetched from your internet banking page.

The accounts are used to segment your transactions.

The accounts are stored indefinitely in the Pengar database.

Social security number Not funny

Your social security number is used to authenticate to Swedbank's mobile internet banking page.

Your social security number is NOT saved. It is kept in volatile memory until the communication with Swedbank's servers is done.

Security code

Your security is treated the same way as your social security number.
